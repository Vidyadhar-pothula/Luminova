Holography

A revolution in visual technology

The Birth of Holography

Holography began with Dennis Gabor in 1947 while working to improve electron microscopy. He developed the concept of "wavefront reconstruction" which became the foundation of holographic technology. This groundbreaking work earned him the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1971.

The practical implementation of holography had to wait until 1960 when Theodore Maiman invented the laser, providing the coherent light source needed to create true holograms.

3D

The Theory

Gabor's theory proposed recording both amplitude and phase information of light waves, unlike photography which only captures intensity. This allows for the recreation of three-dimensional images.

First Holograms

The first successful transmission holograms were created by Emmett Leith and Juris Upatnieks at the University of Michigan in 1962 using the newly invented laser.

Modern Applications

Today, holography is used in security features, data storage, medical imaging, and art. The technology continues to evolve with new applications emerging in augmented reality.
